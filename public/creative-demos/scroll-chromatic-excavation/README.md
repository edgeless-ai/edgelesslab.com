# Scroll-Chromatic Excavation

Scroll into the dark to reveal hidden text buried in near-black color — thousands of microscopic agents descend into the void, biased toward the coldest pixels, and burn hidden glyphs into light.

---

## Technique Notes

This piece combines two techniques into a single excavation system:

1. **Scroll-driven reveal:** The canvas is far taller than the viewport. The user scrolls down through a dark void. At the top sits a hidden layer of text rendered in near-black (`#050505`) on a black background (`#000000`). The text is invisible until the excavation agents find it.

2. **Agent-based excavation:** Thousands of autonomous microscopic agents walk the pixel grid. Each agent is biased toward the coldest (darkest) pixels in its neighborhood. When an agent lands on a pixel that is slightly warmer than pure black — a hidden glyph — it "burns" it into light by raising the pixel's brightness. Over time, the agents collectively excavate the buried text into visibility.

3. **Rebel agents (3%):** A small fraction of agents are rebels. Instead of descending toward darkness, they walk toward the light. When they encounter an already-excavated glyph, they erase it back toward black. This creates a living, contested surface — the text is never fully stable.

## How to Interact

| Interaction | Behavior |
|-------------|----------|
| **Scroll** | Descend through the dark void. The excavation happens continuously as you scroll; the agents are always working, but your viewport reveals what they have burned into light. |
| **Mouse** | Repels nearby agents. Push them away from a region and watch the excavation slow or divert. |
| **Click** | Spawns a riot — a burst of new agents at the click location, scrambling the local excavation pattern. |
| **R** | Buries everything. Resets the canvas to black, re-hides the text, and clears all agent trails. |
| **S** | Toggles the scroll indicator on/off. |
