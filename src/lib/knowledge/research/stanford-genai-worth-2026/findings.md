# Findings — What is Generative AI Worth?

## Aggregate U.S. consumer surplus
| Wave | Users | Total Annual CS | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| July 2025 | 98.78M | $116.2B | baseline |
| March 2026 | 115.33M | $172.3B | +48–50% |

- Adoption rate rose from 48% to 56% of U.S. adults
- Most tools remain free or close-to-free for consumers

## Per-user valuations
| Metric | July 2025 | March 2026 | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mean WTA | $98.00 | $124.50 | +27% |
| Median WTA | $3.39 | $11.40–$11.48 | +235–238% |

- Median less susceptible to extreme high valuations
- 50% of users would accept ≤$2.27 in 2025 vs ≤$11.40 in 2026
- Dec 2024 pilot (N=502): median WTA = $0.52 → confirms negligible valuations pre-mainstream adoption

## Producer vs consumer capture
- 2025 global consumer revenue for leading gen AI firms: $14.2B
- Consumer surplus vs producer revenue: ~12×
- Aligns with Nordhaus (2004): innovators capture ~3% of total social returns from major 20th century advances

## Adoption landscape (from AI Index 2026, integrated context)
- Generative AI reached 53% adoption in 3 years — fastest recent general-purpose technology
- U.S. ranks 24th globally in gen AI adoption at 28.3% despite leading in investment/model development
- China & Europe posted highest YoY increases in organizational AI adoption

## Productivity gains
- Customer support: 14–15%
- Software development: 26%
- Marketing output: 50%
- Gains smaller for deeper reasoning tasks
- Emerging concern: heavy AI reliance may carry long-term learning penalties

## Labor market
- Employment for software developers aged 22–25 fell nearly 20% from 2024 to 2025
- 1/3 of employers expect further workforce reductions in coming year
- Anticipated reductions highest in service operations, supply chain, software engineering

## Why median ≠ mean matters
A small fraction of heavy users has WTA in the hundreds of dollars. That pulls the mean to $124.50 while the median sits at $11.40. For policy or product targeting, median is the more honest central tendency.
