# What is Generative AI Worth? — Paper Profile

- **Authors:** Erik Brynjolfsson, Avinash Collis, Felix Eggers, Sophia Kazinnik, David Nguyen
- **Affiliation:** Stanford Digital Economy Lab
- **Release:** April 13, 2026
- **Type:** Working paper
- **PDF:** https://digitaleconomy.stanford.edu/app/uploads/2026/04/WhatsGenAIWorth.pdf
- **HAI AI Index reference:** https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2026-ai-index-report/economy

## One-line summary
U.S. consumer surplus from generative AI grew from $116B to $172B in 8 months (July 2025 → March 2026) via willingness-to-accept surveys, implying consumers capture ~12× more value than producers.

## Research question
What is the welfare gain to U.S. consumers from generative AI tools (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Copilot), measured independently of GDP or revenue metrics?

## Core method
Online choice experiments on Prolific, representative U.S. adult samples matched to census demographics. Respondents are asked the compensation they would accept to lose one month of access to any gen AI tool.

## Key numbers
| Metric | July 2025 | March 2026 | Change |
|---|---|---|---|

| Median WTA per user | $3.39 | $11.40–$11.48 | +235–238% |
| U.S. adult users | 98.78M | 115.33M | +21% |
| Aggregate annual CS | $116.2B | $172.3B | +48–50% |

## Verdict for Edgeless
Directly relevant to "AI value capture" narrative. The 12× consumer-to-producer surplus ratio is the killer stat. Median WTA ($11.40) vs mean ($124.50) is a useful reminder that most users value gen AI modestly while power users value it extremely.
